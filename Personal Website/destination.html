Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo is the capital of Japan and one of the most populous cities in the world, with a population of over 14 million residents as of 2023 and the second most populated capital in the world. The Greater Tokyo Area, which includes Tokyo and parts of six neighboring prefectures, is the most-populous metropolitan area in the world, with 41 million residents as of 2024.

Located at the head of Tokyo Bay, Tokyo is part of the Kantō region on the central coast of Honshu, Japan's largest island.

San Francisco, California

San Francisco is a vibrant city located on the west coast of the United States, known for its iconic landmarks like the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, and its historic cable cars. With its rolling hills and diverse neighborhoods, it offers a blend of culture, innovation, and natural beauty. As a hub for technology and entrepreneurship, it’s part of the larger Silicon Valley region. The city's coastal location also provides a cool, foggy climate, which locals affectionately call "Karl the Fog." San Francisco is a cultural melting pot, celebrated for its arts, food, and progressive spirit.

Mexico City

Mexico City is a dynamic metropolis located in the heart of Mexico, known for its rich history, from pre-Hispanic times to its current role as the country’s cultural and economic center. Its most iconic landmarks include the Zócalo, the Palace of Fine Arts, and the ancient ruins of Teotihuacán in its surrounding areas. With a vibrant cultural diversity, the city offers a unique blend of traditions and modernity, reflected in its cuisine, art, and nightlife. Despite being at a high altitude, its temperate climate attracts both residents and visitors. Additionally, it serves as a major hub for business and politics in Latin America.

Mazatlán

Mazatlán is a city located on the Pacific coast of Mexico, known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and rich cultural heritage. It is famous for its annual Carnival, one of the largest and most colorful in Latin America. With a warm climate and an array of outdoor activities, it’s a popular destination for tourists seeking sun and sea. The city also has a historic center with charming colonial architecture and a bustling waterfront promenade.

New York City

New York City, often called "The Big Apple," is a global hub for finance, culture, and entertainment. Located on the east coast of the United States, it's known for its towering skyline, anchored by landmarks like the Statue of Liberty, Times Square, and the Empire State Building. The city is a melting pot of cultures, offering world-class dining, theater, and art in districts like Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens. As a center for business and innovation, it's home to Wall Street and countless global companies. New York City is also famous for its 24/7 energy, making it one of the most iconic cities in the world.
